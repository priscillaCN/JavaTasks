To count your menstrual cycle, count from the first day of bleeding (Day 1) to the day before your next period begins. A normal cycle ranges from 21 to 35 days, with 28 days being average. Use a calendar or tracking app to mark the start date of each period to determine your average length over a few months.  
Steps to Track Your Cycle

* Identify Day 1: The first day of full, bright red bleeding is day one of your cycle.
* Mark the Calendar: Use a calendar or app to mark the first day of your period with an "X".
* Count the Days: Count every day, starting with day 1, until the day before your next period begins.
* Calculate Length: The total number of days is your cycle length.
* Example: If your period starts on July 4 and the next one starts on July 29, your cycle is 24 days (count July 4 through July 27; July 28 is when the next cycle starts).

Key Considerations

* Normal Range: A regular cycle is typically 21 to 35 days.
* Irregularity: Cycles shorter than 21 days or longer than 35 days are considered irregular and should be discussed with a doctor.
* Ovulation: Ovulation often occurs around 14 days before the next period.
* Fertile Window: The most fertile days are usually 5 days before ovulation and the day of ovulation. 
Tracking your cycle helps identify your fertility window, track ovulation, and monitor for irregularities.
